Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We wish to submit our manuscript entitled **"Season-Structured Validation Architecture for Prescriptive Analytics: An LLM-Augmented Research Pipeline"** for consideration for publication in *MDPI Analytics*.

The paper presents a season-structured validation architecture for prescriptive analytics research. A locally deployed large language model proposes directional cryptocurrency strategies within experimental seasons, while a multi-layer validation system and a human reviewer at season boundaries constrain the search and update objectives and acceptance gates. We believe the work is a strong fit for *Analytics* because it bridges predictive modelling, simulation-based optimisation, and temporally-ordered feature validation into a single reproducible decision-support system, and it provides a disciplined treatment of look-ahead bias, overfitting, and multiple testing in automated strategy research.

Our key contributions are:

1. A mixed-initiative architecture that confines full autonomy to experimental seasons and reserves human oversight for season transitions.
2. A layered validation protocol combining syntax checking, AST-based future-leak detection, leakage-free feature-catalog compliance, and strict out-of-sample evaluation.
3. Empirical evidence across 34 333 candidate strategies and 329 accepted strategies, including a selected BNB/USDT case study and a cross-asset replication on BTC/USDC, with bootstrap diagnostics and an explicit discussion of the Q1 2026 post-selection observation.
4. A lightweight ablation study that shows the pipeline's scientific value lies in systematic validation rather than in uniquely superior LLM feature selection, together with an explicit discussion of limitations and the distinction between passive holdout, secondary selection holdout, and cross-season generalisation.

We confirm that neither the manuscript nor any parts of its content are currently under consideration for publication with or published in another journal. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to *Analytics*.

We thank you for your consideration.
